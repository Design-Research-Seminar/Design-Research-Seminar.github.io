What?
The seminar Frontieres of Design, is a seminar of the Master 2 - Design research organised by three school (ENS, Telecom, ENSCI) and focus on presenting a variety of different research work that are happening at the edge of design and research. This seminar start in 2020 during the covid crisis and will be held online on zoom. The seminar is dedicated to the Master 2 students but open to a broader public.
FUTURE TALKS
Design and imaginaries: New ways to think, and new ways to live - 10 decembre 2020 - 15h-17h

Abstract
In this talk we'll look at the idea of imaginaries—the mental images and understandings people have of concepts, from the climate crisis to our own health. We'll talk about how designers can work with imaginaries, to uncover them, help people share them, and help people reimagine how life could be, including through designing new kinds of more "qualitative" interfaces for technology, and exploring ways to materialise qualitative data through metaphors and other approaches. I'll share examples of 'research through design' projects with my students, such as current and recent projects with the Festival of Maintenance, Demos Helsinki, and Philips, speculative design around climate, new kinds of design tools for understanding ourselves, and how we can use new metaphors to think about new kinds of future for ourselves and our communities.

Biography
Dr Dan Lockton is a designer and researcher, specialising in people’s interactions with technology, and how that affects the way we think, act, and understand. Dan is currently Assistant Professor of Design at Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, USA, and will be joining TU Eindhoven's Future Everyday group, in the Netherlands, in early 2021. He runs the Imaginaries Lab, an international creative research studio using design methods to address social and environmental challenges that humanity—and the planet—face, through collaborations around new interfaces with technology, climate and energy transitions, health and wellbeing, and esign and creativity methods. Dan previously worked for the Royal College of Art in London. Twitter: @imaginari_es Website: http://imaginari.es

Dr Marc Teyssier - Anthropomorphic Devices - 17 decembre 2020 - 14h-16h

Abstract
Skin-On Interfaces are sensitive skin-like input methods than can be added to existing devices to increase their capabilities. When we interact with others, we use skin as interfaces. However the objects of mediated communication - such as the smartphone - still has a cold interface that doesn't allow natural interaction and input. In this project, I wanted to make available the perfect human interface that is the skin for existing devices.

Biography
Interaction designer and researcher, I develop creative hardware and software experiences that bring technology closer to human nature. I am currently postdoctoral researcher at Saarland University, I did my PHD thesis at Télécom Paris and HCI La Sorbonne. Get my Resume. These days, I use critical design to question existing devices form factor and explore how communication can be mediated through anthropomorphic devices.

Dr Joanna Boehnert - Design with Intent

Abstract
Despite accumulative social and technological innovation, the design industry continues to face significant obstacles when addressing issues of sustainability. Climate change and other systemic ecological problems demands shifts on an order of magnitude well beyond the trajectory of business-as-usual. I will argue that these complex problems require addressing the epistemological error in knowledge systems reproducing unsustainable designed worlds. Ecological literacy is a basis for nature-inspired design. Ecologically engaged knowledge must inform design strategies across the psychological, the social and the environmental domains. With the expansive three ecologies perspective, interventions at the intersection of design and economics can enable systems transitions. This theoretical work informs a framing of the current epoch in ways that create a foundation for the creation of regenerative, distributed and redirected design economies.

Biography
I completed an AHRC-funded PhD in Design at the University of Brighton in 2012. I gained an MA from the University of Arts London and a BA Honours with distinction at the University of Guelph, Ontario, Canada.

I founded EcoLabs in London in 2006 as a design research studio engaged with the visual communication of complex environmental problems. Three years later, my PhD research proposal describing why the design industry needed ecologically literate design theory was awarded AHRC funding. I completed a doctorate titled The Visual Communication of Ecological Literacy: Designing, Learning and Emergent Ecological Perception under the supervision of Professor of Sustainable Design Jonathan Chapman and Professor of Media Studies Julie Doyle.

Post-PhD, I conducted a practice-based research project called ‘Mapping Climate Communication’ during a CIRES Visiting Fellowship at Center for Science and Technology Policy Research (CSTPR) in the Cooperative Institute for Research in Environmental Sciences (CIRES) at the University of Colorado Boulder. Next, I wrote my book Design, Ecology, Politics: Toward the Ecocene while working as a Research Fellow at the Centre for Research and Education in Arts and Media (CREAM) at the University of Westminster. Immediately before joining Loughborough University, I led a research project on the visual communication of complexity at the Centre for the Evaluation of Complexity Across the Nexus (CECAN) at the University of Surrey.

As an environmental communication designer, I have worked in a variety of spaces in academia, the creative industries, publishing, and non-profit and activist organisations. I co-founded Transition Town Brixton (2007) and Occupy Design UK (2011) and have worked with many groups on issues of environmental and social justice. You can follow my latest activities on my academic Twitter account @ecocene and my personal blog.

Call for Proposal (IN CONSTRUCTION)
We are soliciting *submissions on abstracts* (up to 2 pages), which present original work. These can be finalized research works, but also ongoing research, ANR, European or other projects, industrial feedback, etc. It can also be work that has been submitted but not accepted, and presented in order to improve it for a new submission. You can submit your work anytime during the year, we will take the time to read it and see if it's fit to the need of our students.

Topics
All topics related to design are interesting for us. We also value novel topics at the frontiers or the border of the domains. Below you can find some topics of interest :
* design and substainability * design and regulation * critical/speculative/fictional design * Research in design * Design for research * interaction design * visualization design * novel practices in design * novel methods in design * industrial design * graphic design * Study of design
